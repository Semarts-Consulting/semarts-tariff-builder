# Asset Valuation Methodology Decision Pack

## Purpose

Define the decisions required before asset evidence can calculate tariff-driving annual asset recovery amounts.

Recommended default: asset records remain evidence-only unless an annual amount is explicitly approved or generated by a separately approved valuation method. The current tariff-driving path remains explicit cost pools and allocation methods.

## Current Position

Tariff Builder can import and review asset evidence, including:

- asset description;
- asset category;
- electrical distribution asset flag;
- electricity tariff chargeability flag;
- voltage;
- network level;
- life years;
- prior-year asset value.

Asset evidence is currently visible for review and reporting readiness. It does not calculate annuity, depreciation, recoverable cost, allocation, tariff rates or report totals automatically.

WB-005 already approved test-only coverage using pre-set annual asset amounts. That approval does not approve production asset valuation logic.

## Decision Modes

| Mode | Meaning | Tariff impact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Asset evidence-only | Asset rows support review but do not create cost pools. | Current approved mode. |
| Approved annual amount | User-reviewed annual asset amount can become an explicit cost pool. | Allowed only when represented as explicit `CostPoolRow`. |
| Calculated annual amount | Application calculates annual recovery from asset value, life, WACC, CPI or depreciation method. | Blocked until methodology approval. |

## Asset Treatment Categories

A future implementation should distinguish:

- chargeable electrical distribution assets;
- chargeable metering or local network assets;
- non-electrical assets;
- non-chargeable assets;
- shared-use or mixed-use assets;
- unresolved or low-confidence asset rows;
- evidence-only assets.

Only explicitly approved chargeable asset rows should be eligible for tariff recovery.

## Valuation Methods Requiring Approval

The following methods are not approved by this pack, but should be considered before production implementation:

| Method | Description | Main risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Direct annual amount | User enters or imports an already-approved annual asset recovery amount. | Lowest risk, but requires source evidence and approval. |
| Straight-line depreciation | Asset value divided by useful life or remaining life. | Age, remaining life and replacement policy may be disputed. |
| Annuity method | Calculates annual recovery using asset value, life and allowed rate of return. | WACC, inflation, timing and formula assumptions are commercially material. |
| Indexed asset value | Adjusts prior-year value using CPI or other index before recovery calculation. | Index source, timing and compounding need approval. |
| Manual override | User-approved annual amount with rationale. | Requires audit trail and review controls. |

Recommended first production route: allow approved annual asset amounts to create explicit cost-pool candidates before implementing formula-based valuation.

## Required Methodology Decisions

Before formula-based asset valuation is implemented, the owner should decide:

1. Which asset categories are chargeable through electricity tariffs.
2. Whether chargeability depends on asset category, voltage, network level, site use, tenant use or manual review.
3. Whether annual asset recovery should use direct annual amounts, depreciation, annuity, indexed values or manual approval.
4. What source value is used: prior-year asset value, replacement value, net book value, gross book value or manually approved value.
5. Whether useful life, remaining life or standard life should be used.
6. Whether WACC, CPI, inflation or indexation applies.
7. Whether asset annual amounts are allocated by voltage, customer class, consumption, capacity, meter count, direct assignment or manual share.
8. How shared-use, mixed-use, non-electrical and unresolved assets are excluded or reviewed.
9. Whether non-chargeable or unresolved asset evidence blocks report readiness.
10. What audit evidence must appear in methodology reports.

## Recommended Production Boundary

Future implementation should not make `calculateTariffs` read raw asset rows.

Recommended flow:

1. Review asset evidence.
2. Classify chargeability and methodology treatment.
3. Approve or calculate annual asset recovery amount.
4. Generate a reviewed asset recovery cost-pool candidate.
5. Convert approved candidates into explicit `CostPoolRow` records.
6. Require allocation methods through the existing allocation workflow.
7. Run `calculateTariffs` through the existing cost-pool and allocation path.

This keeps the tariff engine stable and preserves the existing audit trace model.

## Validation Expectations

Future implementation should validate:

- missing asset description;
- missing category;
- invalid voltage or network level;
- invalid or missing life years where formula-based valuation is used;
- negative asset value;
- chargeable asset without valuation treatment;
- formula-based valuation without required assumptions;
- WACC or CPI values outside approved range;
- duplicate asset source mapping;
- shared-use assets without allocation or exclusion decision;
- approved annual asset amount without cost-pool category;
- generated asset recovery cost pool without allocation method.

## Double-Counting Controls

Future implementation must prevent:

- the same asset row creating more than one cost pool unless explicitly split;
- asset evidence being included both as asset recovery and as a direct/overhead cost;
- non-chargeable assets entering recoverable cost pools;
- unresolved or low-confidence rows becoming tariff-driving;
- annual asset amounts being recalculated and also manually entered without reconciliation;
- asset recovery being included in both network charge and pass-through supply charge treatment.

## Reporting Expectations

Reports should be able to show:

- total asset evidence value;
- chargeable and non-chargeable asset value;
- approved annual asset recovery amount;
- excluded, evidence-only and unresolved asset totals;
- valuation method used;
- valuation assumptions such as life, WACC, CPI or manual approval rationale;
- cost-pool category and allocation readiness;
- variance between asset evidence value and annual tariff recovery amount.

Report totals should not change until approved annual asset recovery amounts are represented as explicit tariff-driving cost pools.

## Out Of Scope

This pack does not approve:

- production asset valuation implementation;
- changes to `calculateTariffs`;
- changes to asset import parser output shapes;
- storage schema changes;
- shared DTO changes;
- report total changes;
- export DTO changes;
- UI workflows for valuation approval;
- WACC, CPI, depreciation or annuity formulas;
- automatic conversion of asset evidence into tariff cost pools.

## Recommended Decision

Approve this control direction:

- asset evidence remains evidence-only by default;
- direct approved annual asset amount is the safest first production route;
- formula-based valuation should be a later package after business methodology approval;
- approved asset recovery should become explicit `CostPoolRow` records;
- allocation methods remain mandatory before tariff approval;
- raw asset rows should not be read directly by `calculateTariffs`.

## Acceptance Criteria For Future Implementation

A future implementation proposal must define:

- exact chargeability categories;
- supported valuation method or methods;
- required assumptions and source data;
- approval and manual override workflow;
- mapping from approved annual amount to cost-pool category;
- allocation readiness rules;
- report and audit evidence;
- tests proving non-chargeable, unresolved, evidence-only and duplicate asset rows cannot affect tariff revenue requirement.
